06 — Summary

Key Findings

Core insights derived from the EDA pipeline on the HAM10000 dataset.

⚠️
Extreme Class Imbalance The NV class accounts for 66.9% of the dataset — 58× the size of the smallest class (DF, 115 images). Oversampling, weighted loss, or augmentation is required.
🔬
Melanoma Represents Only 11.1% Despite being the most dangerous skin cancer, MEL has only 1,113 samples. High recall on this class is critical for clinical safety.
👨
Male Patient Majority The dataset is skewed toward male patients, which may limit generalizability for female patients without careful evaluation.
📍
Back & Lower Extremity Most Common Lesions cluster most heavily on the back and lower extremities — consistent with sun exposure habits and dermatological biology.
🎂
Dominant Age Group: 40–60 Years Median patient age is 45. Older patients carry higher risk for malignant lesion types such as MEL and BCC.
🖼️
10,015 Images — International Benchmark HAM10000 is the largest and most widely used dermatological dataset in the medical Computer Vision research community.
